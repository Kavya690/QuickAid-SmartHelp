Road & collision response

  1. 1Ensure scene is safe — turn on hazard lights and move away from traffic.
  2. 2Check for injuries — do not move the injured person unless there is immediate danger.
  3. 3Call emergency services: 112 (India).
  4. 4If trained, perform CPR if the person is unresponsive and not breathing.
  5. 5Control any bleeding with firm pressure using a clean cloth.
  6. 6Keep the injured person warm and calm until help arrives.

100–120 compressions/min

Follow these steps carefully until help arrives

  1. 1Check scene is safe. Tap shoulders — shout "Are you okay?"
  2. 2Call 112 immediately or ask someone nearby to call.
  3. 3Place heel of hand on centre of chest. Interlock fingers. Keep arms straight.
  4. 4Push down 5–6 cm at 100–120 compressions/min. Count aloud to keep rhythm.
  5. 5Give 2 rescue breaths after every 30 compressions (if trained).
  6. 6Continue until help arrives or the person responds.

Heimlich Manoeuvre

  1. 1Ask "Are you choking?" — if they can't speak, cough, or breathe, act immediately.
  2. 2Stand behind them, wrap your arms around their waist.
  3. 3Make a fist with one hand just above the navel, thumb inward.
  4. 4Grasp the fist with the other hand. Give quick, upward thrusts.
  5. 5Repeat until the object is dislodged or emergency help arrives.

First Response

  1. 1Cool the burn under running cool water for 10–20 minutes.
  2. 2Do NOT use ice, butter, or toothpaste — they worsen burns.
  3. 3Remove jewelry/clothing near the burn (unless stuck to skin).
  4. 4Cover with a clean, non-fluffy material (cling film or clean plastic bag).
  5. 5Call 112 for severe/large burns, facial burns, or chemical burns.

Immobilize & Support

  1. 1Stop the person from moving the injured area.
  2. 2Support the limb above and below the suspected fracture.
  3. 3Apply a splint if available — do not straighten the limb.
  4. 4Apply ice packs wrapped in cloth to reduce swelling.
  5. 5Seek immediate medical attention or call 112.

Wound Care

  1. 1Apply firm, direct pressure with a clean cloth or bandage.
  2. 2Keep pressure for at least 10 minutes without lifting the cloth.
  3. 3Elevate the injured part above heart level if possible.
  4. 4If bleeding soaks through, add more cloth on top — do not remove the first layer.
  5. 5For severe/uncontrolled bleeding, call 112 immediately.

Recognize & Treat

  1. 1Signs: pale/clammy skin, rapid weak pulse, confusion, rapid breathing.
  2. 2Lay the person flat and elevate legs about 30cm (unless head/neck injury suspected).
  3. 3Keep them warm with a blanket. Loosen tight clothing.
  4. 4Do NOT give food or water.
  5. 5Call 112 immediately. Monitor breathing until help arrives.

Evacuation Steps

  1. 1Activate the fire alarm if available. Shout "FIRE!" to alert others.
  2. 2Call 101 (Fire) immediately.
  3. 3Leave immediately — do NOT collect belongings. Close doors behind you.
  4. 4Feel doors before opening — if hot, find another exit.
  5. 5Stay low if there is smoke — crawl to exit. Smoke rises, air is cleaner near the floor.
  6. 6Use stairs ONLY — never use elevators.
  7. 7Assemble at the designated muster point. Never go back inside.

  1. 1Stay calm. Move toward a crowded, well-lit area immediately.
  2. 2Call 112 or Women Helpline 1091 if in danger.
  3. 3Share your live location with a trusted person.
  4. 4Use the fake call feature below to exit an uncomfortable situation discreetly.

During the Earthquake

  1. 1DROP to hands and knees immediately.
  2. 2COVER your head and neck under a sturdy table or against an interior wall.
  3. 3HOLD ON until shaking stops. Do NOT run outside.
  4. 4Stay away from windows, glass, and heavy furniture.
  5. 5If in bed — stay in bed, cover head with pillow.

After the Earthquake

  1. 1Expect aftershocks. Check for injuries — don't move badly injured people.
  2. 2Check for gas leaks — if you smell gas, open windows and leave immediately.
  3. 3Do not use elevators. Be careful of fallen objects on staircases.
  4. 4Turn on radio for emergency broadcast information.
